What changes the guide price

  • Property size and amount of glass
  • Height, storeys, and access
  • Condition and cleaning frequency
  • Extras like gutters, fascias, conservatory roofs, interiors, or solar panels

Why Prices Vary

Two houses in Colchester can look similar and still take very different amounts of time.

The amount of glass, the style of windows, the access, and how regularly the place is cleaned all change the job. That is why this page talks about guide prices rather than pretending there is one flat rate for everything.

More glass means more time

French doors, bifolds, skylights, and extra panels all add up quickly.

Access changes everything

Height, awkward rear access, conservatories, and delicate areas all affect the quote.

First cleans are often slower

A heavily overdue first clean can take noticeably longer than a regular maintenance visit.

What Affects The Quote

The six main things I am usually looking at.

Property size

Smaller homes, larger homes, and properties with lots of glazing need different amounts of time.

Amount of glass

Window count matters, but so do French doors, bifolds, skylights, and conservatory panels.

Height and storeys

Single-storey access is very different from mixed-height or three-storey work.

Access

Side access, rear obstacles, delicate areas, and parking all affect what is practical.

Condition

Regular upkeep is quicker than a first clean where everything has been left for a while.

Extras

Gutters, fascia and soffit cleaning, conservatory roofs, interiors, and solar panels are priced separately.

Regular Cleans Vs First Cleans

Regular work is usually better value than catching up from scratch.

Regular visits usually take less time because the dirt never gets as heavy. That is why guide prices often look better on a regular round than on a one-off or overdue first clean.

Regular cleans

Usually the smoothest option for keeping the windows looking right all year.

First cleans

Can take longer because there is more built-up dirt and more detail work to get through.

Why 6-Weekly Often Works Well

It is often the sweet spot between keeping on top of things and keeping the cost sensible.

Plenty of customers choose 6-weekly because it keeps the windows looking tidy without becoming too frequent. It is not the only option, but it often works well for regular residential window cleaning in Colchester.

4-weekly

Good if you like the windows kept especially tidy.

6-weekly

Often the best balance for family homes and regular local rounds.

8-weekly

Still regular, just a bit more spread out.
